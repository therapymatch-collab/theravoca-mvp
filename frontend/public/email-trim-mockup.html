A reference number on its own isn't sensitive; it can't be matched back to you without a one-click portal sign-in.
PHI in current email: the entire intake form. Free-text fields (prior therapy notes, "anything else") are particularly sensitive -- patients sometimes share trauma history, relationship details, mental health history.
After trim: reference number + sign-in CTA. The reference number alone is not PHI (it's a UUID prefix that can't be re-identified). Full receipt remains accessible at /portal/patient/request/{id} behind the same auto-login token.

Implementation notes

Pages required (some may exist):
  • /portal/therapist/referral/{request_id} — therapist landing for a specific referral. Already exists for the apply flow; add the brief details that came out of the email.
  • /portal/patient/matches — patient sees their matches. Already exists.
  • /portal/patient/request/{id} — patient view of their submitted intake answers. Likely needs to be built; today the data is only viewable in the email.
Auto-login tokens: Every email already uses HMAC-signed magic links per routes/portal.py. The same token can sign in the patient or therapist on click. No new auth machinery required.
Defense in depth: Even with a Resend BAA in place, trimming reduces exposure if a recipient's inbox is breached, the email is forwarded by mistake, or a bug ever leaks email previews. Principle of least disclosure.
Editable copy stays editable: Each template's subject / heading / greeting / intro / cta_label / footer_note remain admin-editable via Content → Email templates. The trim is structural — the dynamic content sections (intake-answer table, match list) are removed from email_service.py entirely; admin-editable copy lives untouched.
