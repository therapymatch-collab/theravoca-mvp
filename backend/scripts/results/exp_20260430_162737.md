# TheraVoca scoring experiment — text-impact run

_Run id: `exp_20260430_162737`_

**N requests:** 50 (half with rich `other_issue`, half empty) — **N applies:** 250 (5 therapists × 5 message variants per request)

## 1. Patient `other_issue` text vs raw match score

Each request was matched against the SAME live therapist pool with the SAME structural fields. The only difference between the two groups is whether the patient filled in the optional *Anything else?* textarea.

| Group | N | Avg raw match_score | Median | Min | Max |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Empty `other_issue` | 125 | 91.18 | 93 | 72 | 95 |
| Rich `other_issue` | 125 | 90.69 | 95 | 68 | 95 |

**Δ (rich − empty):** **-0.49 points** on the raw match score.

## 2. Therapist apply-message vs apply_fit (LLM-graded 0-5)

Five message variants were rotated across the top-5 therapists for each request, then graded by Claude Sonnet 4.5 against the patient's brief.

| Variant | Avg apply_fit | Median | Min | Max | Avg msg length |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|

## 5. Architectural takeaway

- **Patient `other_issue` free text** is now embedded at request creation and soft-bonused via `matching._score_one` (`other_issue_bonus`, max +6 pts, cosine similarity vs therapist T5/T2). Section 1's Δ measures the realised lift in raw match_score from filling in the textarea.
- **Therapist apply text** has a measurable, monotonic effect via `score_apply_fit` (Section 2). Empty/oneliner replies score near-zero; full-engagement replies that quote the patient's free text and address prior-therapy + style score 3.5-5.
- Cross-tab in Section 3 shows whether the LLM grader rewards therapist replies that engage the patient's free text. Negative Δ on D/E variants when patient text is rich means the grader is correctly penalising replies that ignore the textarea — i.e. our engagement bar moved up.
